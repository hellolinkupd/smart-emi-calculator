Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed monthly payment made towards a loan.

How is home loan EMI calculated?

EMI is calculated using the loan amount, interest rate and repayment tenure.

Can I reduce my EMI?

Yes, by choosing a longer tenure, making a larger down payment, or securing a lower interest rate.

Does prepayment reduce interest?

Yes. Making prepayments reduces the outstanding principal and lowers total interest costs.

What Is a Home Loan EMI?

A Home Loan EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount you pay every month to repay your home loan. Each EMI contains both principal repayment and interest payment.

How EMI Is Calculated

EMI depends on three factors: loan amount, interest rate, and repayment tenure. A higher loan amount or interest rate increases EMI, while a longer tenure lowers EMI but increases total interest.

EMI Formula Explained

EMI = P × R × (1+R)^N ÷ ((1+R)^N − 1), where P is the loan amount, R is the monthly interest rate, and N is the total number of monthly installments.

Factors Affecting EMI

The major factors affecting EMI are loan amount, annual interest rate, repayment tenure, credit score, and lender policies.

Benefits of Using an EMI Calculator

An EMI calculator helps borrowers estimate monthly payments instantly, compare loan options, plan budgets, and understand total interest costs before applying for a loan.

Tips to Reduce Home Loan Interest

You can reduce overall interest by making a larger down payment, choosing lower interest rates, making prepayments, and selecting shorter loan tenures whenever affordable.
